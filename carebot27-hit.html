✦ Critical Context · Read Before Asking
⚠️ She finished the most cited mathematical reference work in history. It is known by his name first.
Irene Ann Stegun (1919–2008) co-edited the Handbook of Mathematical Functions with Milton Abramowitz at the National Bureau of Standards. With over 40,000 citations — cited once every 1.5 hours of every working day through the 1990s — it is arguably the most cited scientific reference work ever produced. When Abramowitz died of a heart attack in July 1958, Stegun took over management of the entire project and carried it to completion, publishing in 1964. She was awarded the Department of Commerce Gold Medal in 1965. The book is universally known as "Abramowitz and Stegun" — his name first, always. On the Dover paperback, her name was misprinted as "Irene A. Segun." The handbook is described as "one of the very few scientific activities of the 1950s led by a woman." You would not know that from its name.
