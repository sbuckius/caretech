✦ Critical Context · Read Before Watching
⚠️ Karen Diamond operates the machine for the entire film. She is named once.
This 1967 instructional film was produced by the University of Michigan Computing Center to teach students how to use the IBM 29 Card Punch. Professor Geller narrates the entire 20-minute film. He explains every step. He describes every key. Karen Diamond does every single thing he describes. She feeds the cards. She punches the columns. She backspaces. She duplicates. She clears the track. She is on screen for the majority of the film. She is introduced in a single sentence: "Karen Diamond will assist us by operating the card punch." The film is not about Karen Diamond. The film could not exist without Karen Diamond.
